# AI Writing Detection Patterns

Common AI writing patterns to watch for and avoid when creating or reviewing SEO content. Search engines increasingly detect and potentially discount AI-generated content that lacks genuine expertise and originality.

## Overused Transition Phrases

These phrases appear far more frequently in AI-generated text than in human writing:

- "In today's digital landscape"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "In conclusion"
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's explore"
- "Without further ado"
- "At the end of the day"
- "It goes without saying"
- "In the realm of"
- "Navigating the complexities of"
- "Unlocking the potential of"

## Punctuation Patterns

- **Em dashes** — AI text overuses em dashes compared to human writing
- **Semicolons** — unusually frequent semicolons in short paragraphs
- **Exclamation marks** — forced enthusiasm in informational content

## Structural Patterns

- Every paragraph has exactly 3-4 sentences
- Every section follows the same template (intro → point → example → conclusion)
- Headers are overly symmetrical (all the same length, all the same structure)
- Lists always have exactly 3 or 5 items
- Every section ends with a summary sentence

## Vocabulary Signals

Words and phrases that appear disproportionately in AI text:

- "Leverage" (instead of "use")
- "Utilize" (instead of "use")
- "Delve" / "Delve into"
- "Crucial" / "Pivotal" / "Paramount"
- "Comprehensive" / "Robust" / "Seamless"
- "Landscape" (metaphorical)
- "Tapestry"
- "Embark on a journey"
- "Foster" (outside of parenting context)
- "Multifaceted"
- "Nuanced"
- "Streamline"

## Content Depth Signals

- Generic advice without specific examples
- No original data, research, or firsthand experience
- Covers breadth but never depth
- Claims expertise but shows no evidence of it
- Recycles commonly available information without adding value

## What to Do Instead

- Use specific examples from real experience
- Include original data or research when possible
- Write in the natural voice of the author
- Vary sentence and paragraph length
- Use simple, direct language
- Add genuine opinions and perspectives
- Reference specific tools, processes, or workflows actually used
- Include details that only someone with real experience would know
